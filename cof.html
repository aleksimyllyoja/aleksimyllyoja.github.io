Modern Take on Computer Output on Film

Motivation

Options to save hard copies from digital data are very much limited. Especially when dealing with images, more or less the only easily approachable one is a printer or a plotter. There exists too few output devices, and more is desperately needed, for purposes of creating art, if nothing else.

One underappreciated method, but maybe the most interesting one, is controllably shooting photons to a photographic paper or a film.1

Work

An OLED-panel, controlled by a Raspberry PI, was used to expose photographic paper with computer generated / drawn images for 0.1-0.2 seconds. 2
